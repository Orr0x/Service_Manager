## Executive Summary

The Field Service Management (FSM) market represents a $5.2B opportunity characterized by fragmented solutions and a critical gap: only 40% of field service work is managed through existing software. The remaining 60% involving subcontractors operates through manual processes (emails, calls, PDFs), creating significant inefficiency and visibility challenges.

Our analysis reveals a substantial market opportunity for a network-enabled FSM platform that transforms subcontracting from manual coordination to digital collaboration. With 64% of service businesses planning to increase technology investment post-2020, market timing is optimal.

## Market Dynamics

### Key Drivers

1. **Labor Shortage Intensification**
   - 78% of contractors report difficulty finding skilled workers
   - Subcontracting volume increased 34% since 2020
   - Need for efficient subcontractor management is critical

2. **Digital Acceleration Post-COVID**
   - 71% of field service organizations accelerated digital transformation
   - Contactless operations became standard expectation
   - Real-time visibility now mandatory, not optional

3. **Complex Project Requirements**
   - Multi-trade coordination increased 45%
   - Regulatory compliance requirements more stringent
   - Client expectations for transparency and reporting

### Market Pain Points

**Primary Challenges:**

2. **Scheduling Inefficiencies** (87% of respondents)
3. **Payment Processing Delays** (76% of respondents)
